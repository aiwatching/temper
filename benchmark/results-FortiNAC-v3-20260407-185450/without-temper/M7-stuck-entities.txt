## Summary

Two bugs in `EntityStateMachine.processStage()`:

**Bug 1 — Uncaught exceptions leave entities permanently stuck** (the main issue you're seeing):
- When `txRunner::runPolicy` throws a RuntimeException (NPE, connection error, etc.), it propagates out of `processStage()`, skipping the DB update entirely
- Entity stays at `LABELED` with `processingCode=0` forever
- Recovery job finds it, retries, same exception → infinite loop
- **Fix**: Wrap `serviceFunction.apply(dto)` in try-catch, converting exceptions to `SYSTEM_ERROR (-1)`, which triggers the retry mechanism. After 3 retries, the error code is persisted and the entity is marked as failed.

**Bug 2 — Stage advances unconditionally, even on failure**:
- `dto.setProcessingStage(nextStage)` ran regardless of success/failure
- A failed policy evaluation would show `stage=EVALUATED, code=3200` — misleading since evaluation never succeeded
- **Fix**: Only call `setProcessingStage(nextStage)` when `serviceSuccess=true`. Failed entities now stay at the stage where they failed (e.g., `LABELED` with `code=3200`), making it clear where the problem is.
